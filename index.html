"The Penn State Westgate Building, originally opened in 2004 as the Information Sciences and Technology Building and later renamed in 2017 to better reflect its role as both a home for the College of Information Sciences and Technology (IST) and a shared space with the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, is one of the most striking and ambitious architectural landmarks on the University Park campus, designed by Rafael Viñoly Architects in collaboration with Perfido Weiskopf Architects as a massive structure that quite literally bridges Atherton Street, symbolizing connection while also physically linking east and west campus; inspired by Florence’s Ponte Vecchio, the 199,000-square-foot, \$58.8 million complex feels part academic hub, part campus gateway, offering classrooms with advanced telecommunications and multimedia infrastructure, flexible research labs including a state-of-the-art three-dimensional immersive visualization environment, a 155-seat “cybertorium” for lectures and presentations, open study lounges and collaboration spaces, faculty and staff offices, and a newly renovated academic services suite designed to better serve a growing student population, while at the same time creating a distinctive experiential atmosphere through its use of light, glass, and sweeping interior corridors that give students and faculty views of both the surrounding landscape and the city streets below; though initially criticized for its confusing navigation—having more than a dozen entrances and no clear “front door”—the Westgate Building has since been improved with better signage and wayfinding, yet it still retains a kind of maze-like intrigue that reflects both the complexity and interconnectedness of the disciplines it houses, making it not only a center for digital-age education and interdisciplinary research but also a symbol of Penn State’s embrace of innovation, collaboration, and architectural boldness.
